About Surabhi

Hi, I'm Surabhi Mehrotra

I'm an English Language Trainer, Academic Writer, and qualified Assistant Professor in India, with over 10 years of experience teaching both adolescents and adults. A graduate in English and Communication Studies, I hold certificates from the University Grants Commission and the British Council.

My work focuses on bridging gaps in language proficiency for students of all age groups and working professionals — tailoring every course to specific goals. I've mentored students transitioning to the IB curriculum and moving to schools abroad, particularly in the UK.

I'm deeply passionate about reading across genres, and I strive to help every learner develop a boundless vision and an urge to create — qualities that serve them throughout their lives.

PhD in the UK

My Research on Learning & Emotion

At the University of Essex, I'm investigating how Social and Emotional Learning (SEL) shapes the academic emotions of undergraduate STEM students in India.

This research sits at the intersection of language education, emotional psychology, and STEM pedagogy — with implications for how we design English courses for science students.

  • How SEL-integrated language courses affect achievement emotions (enjoyment, anxiety, boredom)
  • Bridging ESL curriculum design with emotional intelligence frameworks
  • Generating evidence applicable to Indian higher education policy
  • Working under Dr Nasser Jabbari & Dr Christina Gkonou at Essex
PhD Thesis — Ongoing

Exploring the Impact of SEL-Integrated Language Courses on Achievement Emotions of Undergraduate STEM Students in India

A doctoral study at the Department of Language and Linguistics, University of Essex. Investigating how social and emotional learning woven into English language instruction affects how STEM undergraduates feel about — and perform in — their studies.

Supervisors: Dr Nasser Jabbari & Dr Christina Gkonou
Institution: University of Essex, Colchester Campus
